Research Interests (Wow how exciting!):

Computer Security

I have a passion for computer security. Ever since I was first introduced to the field as a sophomore in high school, I have had an obsession with breaking into things and finding what makes them tick. Whereas computer science has become increasingly more prominent in the K-12 curriculum, I find that there has been a disturbing lack of cybersecurity awareness. Most of the bugs and flaws that I have noted in my search for vulnerabilities have been due to blatantly horrific miscues by developers who, often times, do not know any better. Therefore, I am interested in investigating this fine line between maintaining a composed security presence for applications and ensuring a seamless and streamlined experience for end users.

Geospatial Technologies

According to Wikipedia, a geographic information system is "a system designed to capture, store, manipulate, analyze, manage, and present spatial or geographic data." Despite being a relatively under the radar field, geospatial technologies play a monumental role in other more prominent domains such as helping historians map out battles from the Civil War to fighting the opioid epidemic by mapping areas of heavy opioid abuse and placing emergency health resources in more centralized locations. Evidently, geospatial technologies have applications that extend way beyond the simple mapping of surfaces. I hope to use both my expertise in computer science and my interest in GIS to solve demographic and social issues that may stem from a simple lack of information.

Quantum Computing

I have a strong fascination for both quantum physics and its applications to computing, in particular the relationship between information loss and the extraction of work. I find concepts such as Landauer's principle and reversible computing to be absolutely astounding, simply because there is the implication that we can theoretically undergo computation without releasing any heat energy, a prospect that has been tantalizing to a wide variety of researchers. I hope to further my studies in physics, statistics, and linear algebra so that I may be able to continue research on such a promising field.
